How it works

If a child goes missing, their family registers a recent photo here. Years later, anyone who finds or sees a child they believe may be lost or taken can upload a photo. We compare the faces and, when there is a strong match, we alert the registered family.

  1. A family reports a missing child with a clear photo and the date the child went missing.
  2. Someone who finds a child uploads a photo of that child.
  3. Our face-matching compares the photo against every registered child.
  4. If the match is 80% or higher, an alert with the found photo is created for the family.

This is a tool to assist a human search — not a replacement for the police or child-protection authorities. Face matching can be wrong. Any possible match must be verified by people before any action is taken. If you believe a child is in immediate danger, contact your local emergency services first.
