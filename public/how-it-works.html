How it works & how to use this site

Missing Child Finder uses face matching to help reunite missing children with their families. This page explains what the site is for, who it is for, exactly how to use it, and the rules that keep everyone safe.

Our purpose

When a child goes missing, the most powerful tool families have is their photo. But a single photo only helps if the right person sees it at the right moment. This site lets a family register that photo once, so that anyone, anywhere, at any time in the future who finds or sees a child can quietly check whether that child is one a family is searching for — without having to know which family to contact.

Everything here is designed to assist a human search and bring a possible match to people who can verify it safely. It does not replace the police or child-protection authorities.

The four steps

  1. A family reports a missing child with a clear, front-facing photo and the date the child went missing.
  2. Someone who finds or sees a child takes or uploads a photo of that child.
  3. The faces are compared. A face "fingerprint" of the new photo is checked against every registered child.
  4. If the match is 80% or higher, an alert with the found photo is created and the registered family is notified by email or text.

How to use it

If you are a parent or guardian

  1. Open Report a missing child.
  2. Enter the child's name, age when missing, and the date they went missing.
  3. Add your email and/or phone number — this is how we reach you if there is a match.
  4. Add a clear, recent, front-facing photo of the child (one person, well lit).
  5. Submit. Your report stays active so a match can happen years later.

If you have found or seen a child

  1. Open I found a child.
  2. On a phone, tap "Take a photo" to use your camera right then, or "Choose a photo" to use an existing one.
  3. Optionally tap "Use my location" so the family knows where the child was seen.
  4. Submit to compare the photo against every registered child.
  5. If there is a strong match, the family is alerted automatically — do not approach or confront anyone.

How the matching works

The face analysis runs inside your own browser using an open-source library (face-api.js). It finds the face in the photo and turns it into a list of 128 numbers — a "face fingerprint." Only that fingerprint and the photo are sent to our server; the photo is never sent to any third-party AI service.

The server compares fingerprints by measuring how far apart they are and converts that into a percentage. A match of 80% or higher is treated as a strong match and creates an alert. This threshold is set deliberately high to reduce false alarms — but matching can still be wrong.

Photo tips for the best results

  • Use a clear, front-facing photo where the face is well lit.
  • One person in the frame works best; if there are several faces we use the largest.
  • Get reasonably close — a tiny or blurry face cannot be matched reliably.
  • JPG or PNG work everywhere. iPhone "HEIC" photos may need to be exported as JPG first.

Guidelines & safety rules

  • Only upload photos you are entitled to share. Use this site to help find a child — never to track, monitor, or harass anyone.
  • A possible match is not proof. Faces can look alike, and the photo conditions vary. Every match must be checked by people.
  • Do not approach or confront anyone based on a match. Share a strong match with the police or child-protection authorities, who can verify it safely.
  • Keep contact details accurate. A registered family is only reachable if the email or phone on file is correct.
In an emergency, act first. If you believe a child is in immediate danger, contact your local emergency services right away. This site is a help, not a substitute for the authorities.

Privacy

  • Face analysis happens in your browser; the original photo is not sent to any outside AI service.
  • Registered photos and contact details are used solely to help find the child and alert the family.
  • Found-photo uploads create an alert for the matched family so they can follow up through the proper authorities.

Face matching can be wrong, and this tool assists — it does not replace — the police or child-protection authorities. Any possible match must be verified by people before any action is taken.
